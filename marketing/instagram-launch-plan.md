## 1. Do hashtags still pull in an audience?

**Bottom line: No — hashtags are no longer an audience-*pulling* tool. They're a labeling tool.**

- The old mechanism (browsing/following hashtag feeds) is essentially dead. Instagram removed the ability to *follow* hashtags in late 2024.
- The algorithm now decides reach based on the **content itself** (what's in the video/image, audio, caption keywords) and **early engagement** (saves, shares, comments) — not tags.
- What hashtags still do: help Instagram **categorize** your post and catch a little in-app **search** traffic. Real but minor — a ~+5% helper, not the engine.

**Takeaway:** Use 3–5 relevant hashtags (they help categorization, don't hurt), but put 95% of effort into the hook, searchable caption keywords, and content people want to save/share.

### Platform cheat-sheet
- **Instagram:** 3–5 relevant hashtags. Keywords in caption + alt text matter more (IG has real search now).
- **TikTok:** Audio + first-2-second hook + watch time drive reach. 3–5 tags; skip #fyp.
- **YouTube Shorts:** Title, thumbnail, first line of description. Tags barely matter.
- **LinkedIn:** ~3 hashtags.
- **X/Twitter:** 1–2 max.
- **Pinterest:** Keyword-driven (works like search); hashtags mostly deprecated.

## 2. What actually pulls an audience in 2026
1. **Strong hook** — first 1–3 seconds (video) or first line (caption).
2. **Searchable keywords** — write the way people search.
3. **Saves & shares** — weigh more than likes for reach.
4. **Consistency + clear niche** — algorithm rewards recognizable, repeat content.
5. **Native trends** — trending audio, formats, timely topics.
6. **Engaging back** — reply to comments early.

## 3. Vinster audience & positioning

Vinster = a wine companion app. Point your camera at a bottle/label → OCR reads it →
tasting info, vintage quality, pricing, and personalized recommendations.

Two audiences (lead with #1):
1. **Curious novices** — intimidated by wine lists; want to not feel dumb. *Biggest growth audience.*
2. **Enthusiasts/collectors** — care about vintages, regions, value.

Positioning angle: **"Wine without the snobbery."** Stronger than feature lists.

### Content pillars (rotate)
- **Relatable problem:** "POV: 200 bottles, no idea." → scan → instant verdict.
- **Magic-moment demo:** screen-recorded scan → result (<15s). *Most shareable.*
- **Education snackable:** "3 cheap wines that taste expensive," "what 'vintage' means," "reading a French label."
- **Social proof:** a recommendation that nailed someone's taste.

### Format priority
Reels > carousels > single images. Carousels = high saves for educational "swipe to learn."

### Brand styling
Use the app's look: `#E6F4FE` light blue + Cormorant Garamond serif. Elegant, on-brand, consistent.

## 5. CTA strategy: "Follow" vs "Link in bio"

- Instagram does **not** allow tappable links in post captions. Links only work in the **bio**, **Stories** (link sticker), and **DMs**. That's why "link in bio" exists.
- **"Follow" CTA** grows audience (compounds, warms people up → download later).
- **"Link in bio" CTA** drives installs now (higher intent, but loses the not-yet-ready).

**Recommendation:** Use **follow as the active CTA on most posts**, with a harder **"download — link in bio"** every 4th–5th post and in Stories. Always keep the bio link set up as a passive safety net.

### Bio link setup (do this before first post)
Vinster is cross-platform, so don't strand half your users with a single store link. Options:
1. Instagram's native **multi-link** bio feature (Edit Profile → Links, up to 5) — add App Store + Play Store.
2. A free **Linktree / Beacons / Bio.link** page with "Download on iPhone" + "Download on Android" buttons.
3. Later: a smart-link (AppsFlyer Onelink or an `app.vinster.com` redirect) that auto-detects iOS vs Android.

## 7. Two Reel scripts for the scan demo

### Reel A — "The aisle panic" (15s)
- **0–2s (hook, text on screen):** "POV: 200 bottles, zero clue." — film yourself looking overwhelmed in a wine aisle.
- **2–4s:** Pull out phone, open Vinster.
- **4–9s:** Screen-record the scan → result appearing (tasting notes, score, price verdict).
- **9–13s:** Cut back to your face, satisfied nod, grab the bottle.
- **13–15s (CTA text):** "Never guess again. Free — link in bio."
- *Audio:* calm trending sound; on-screen captions throughout (most watch muted).

### Reel B — "Is it worth it?" (12s)
- **0–2s (hook):** "Is this £30 bottle actually good? Let's find out." Hold up a bottle.
- **2–8s:** Screen-record scan → Vinster shows verdict + price comparison.
- **8–10s:** React to the result (pleasantly surprised / "skip it").
- **10–12s (CTA):** "Vinster — sommelier in your pocket. Link in bio."
